About

I am a PhD candidate (ABD) in Economics at the University of Calgary, supervised by Arvind Magesan, Benjamin Crost, and Sacha Kapoor. My research agenda centers on causal evaluations of public policy in low- and middle-income settings, with an emphasis on indigenous (Adivasi) populations in India.

Before beginning the PhD, I worked as a Research Analyst at Outline India and Grail Research, conducting fieldwork on education, water and sanitation, and public health across seven Indian states. That field experience continues to shape both the questions I ask and how I think about data.

Methodologically, I rely on instrumental variables, difference-in-differences, synthetic control, regression discontinuity, and event-study designs, working primarily in Stata, Python, and GIS. A common thread across my projects is the construction of original geocoded datasets that link administrative records, survey microdata, and remote-sensing data.

Job Market Paper

Job Market Paper · 2025

The Impact of Residential Schools on Indigenous Educational Attainment: Evidence from India

Solo-authored · Draft available on request

I estimate the causal effect of Eklavya Model Residential Schools (EMRS) — a federal initiative providing free secondary education to scheduled-tribe students in India — on educational attainment and earnings among school-eligible cohorts. Combining a novel village-level dataset that merges EMRS sanction records with NFHS, IHDS, and remote-sensing data, I exploit cohort eligibility and historical forest cover as instruments for school exposure. Using district and state-by-year fixed effects, I find that EMRS exposure raises years of schooling and shifts long-run labor-market outcomes, with the largest gains concentrated among first-generation learners. The results suggest that targeted residential schooling can meaningfully narrow educational gaps for indigenous populations, even in remote and conflict-affected districts.

Working Papers

Working Papers

  • Public Employment and Political Violence: Evidence from NREGA

    Working paper · 2025

    Using the phased rollout of the National Rural Employment Guarantee Act (NREGA) and close-election variation in district leadership, I evaluate how guaranteed public employment affects insurgent violence in conflict-affected regions of India. The design isolates the political-economy channel from contemporaneous changes in local administrative capacity.

    Draft Slides

  • Cash Transfers, Fertility, and Girls' Education: Evidence from the Bhagyalakshmi Scheme in Karnataka

    Working paper · In progress

    I evaluate Karnataka's Bhagyalakshmi conditional cash transfer, which provides a long-maturity bond to families upon the birth of a girl child, conditional on schooling and health milestones. Using an event-study design around the program's introduction, I examine effects on fertility, girls' educational attainment, and household decision-making.
